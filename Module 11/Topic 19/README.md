# ‚≠ê Limit Order

1. A **Limit Order** is an order to buy or sell a security at a **specified price or better**.
2. Unlike a market order, it gives the trader **control over the execution price**.
3. Limit orders are used to avoid paying more or selling for less than desired.
4. A **buy limit order** executes at the limit price or lower.
5. A **sell limit order** executes at the limit price or higher.
6. Limit orders are not guaranteed to execute if the market does not reach the specified price.
7. They are ideal for **illiquid stocks** where price fluctuations are large.
8. Traders often use limit orders to enter positions at **support or resistance levels**.
9. Limit orders can be placed for **intraday or delivery trading**.
10. They are widely used in **technical trading strategies**, where entry and exit points are predefined.
11. Limit orders reduce **slippage risk** since execution occurs only at desired prices.
12. They help maintain **discipline** by preventing emotional trading decisions.
13. Limit orders are often combined with **stop-loss orders** for effective risk management.
14. Brokers display the **limit orders in the order book**, contributing to market depth.
15. Large limit orders can act as **support or resistance levels** in the market.
16. Traders must ensure **sufficient funds or shares** are available before placing a limit order.
17. Partial execution may occur if the available quantity at the limit price is less than the order quantity.
18. Unfilled portions of limit orders can remain **open until canceled or expired**.
19. Validity options include **Day, IOC, GTC, and GTD**, depending on broker offerings.
20. **Day orders** expire if not executed by market close.
21. **GTC (Good Till Cancelled) orders** remain active until executed or manually canceled.
22. **GTD (Good Till Date) orders** expire on a specified date if not executed.
23. Limit orders are particularly effective in **volatile markets** where prices fluctuate rapidly.
24. Traders often place **buy limit orders** slightly below current market price for better entry.
25. **Sell limit orders** are placed above the market price to capture profits.
26. Limit orders prevent panic buying or selling during market spikes or drops.
27. They allow traders to **automate trade execution** without constant monitoring.
28. In combination with **stop-limit orders**, traders can protect gains and minimize losses.
29. Limit orders contribute to **price discovery** and liquidity in the market.
30. Brokers may charge standard fees, but execution depends on market matching.
31. Investors can view **live order book data** to optimize limit order placement.
32. Large investors often **split limit orders** to avoid market impact.
33. Algorithmic traders frequently use limit orders in **scalping and momentum strategies**.
34. Limit orders provide more **control over trade execution** than market orders.
35. Risk of non-execution exists, especially if the limit is too far from current market price.
36. Traders must balance **desired price with probability of execution**.
37. Limit orders are suitable for low-volatility periods where price stability is high.
38. During earnings announcements, limit orders can prevent **unexpected fills** at unfavorable prices.
39. Limit orders help **institutional investors** manage large trades efficiently.
40. They are also used for **pre-market and post-market sessions** where market orders may not be executed.
41. Investors can **modify or cancel** limit orders before execution.
42. Limit orders support **technical analysis strategies**, such as breakout or reversal trades.
43. They are an essential tool for **swing traders and positional traders**.
44. Brokers display all pending limit orders in the **order book**, providing transparency.
45. Limit orders can be **layered** at multiple price levels to capture different market movements.
46. Effective limit order placement requires understanding **support, resistance, and market trends**.
47. Limit orders reduce the **risk of buying high and selling low**, a common trading mistake.
48. They allow investors to plan trades according to **risk-reward ratios**.
49. Limit orders are critical for disciplined trading, especially in **volatile or thinly traded stocks**.
50. In summary, limit orders give traders **control over price execution**, reduce slippage, and help implement strategic entry and exit plans while accepting that execution is conditional on market price reaching the specified limit.
