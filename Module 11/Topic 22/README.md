1. **IOC (Immediate or Cancel) orders** are executed immediately for the available quantity.
2. Any unfilled portion of an IOC order is automatically **canceled**.
3. IOC orders are used in **volatile or fast-moving markets** to secure immediate execution.
4. They prevent lingering orders that may impact liquidity or pricing.
5. Traders often use IOC for **large-volume trades**, reducing exposure to price fluctuations.
6. IOC orders help capture **quick market opportunities** without waiting for full execution.
7. These orders are commonly used in **arbitrage and high-frequency trading** strategies.
8. **GTT (Good Till Triggered) orders** remain active until a **predefined trigger price** is reached.
9. GTT orders allow investors to **automate trades** without constantly monitoring the market.
10. Once the trigger price is reached, the order is converted into a **market or limit order**.
11. GTT orders are ideal for **long-term investors or swing traders**.
12. They can be used for **buying dips** or **selling rallies** automatically.
13. GTT reduces the need to monitor intraday fluctuations manually.
14. **Cover Orders (CO)** combine a **market order** with a **mandatory stop-loss order**.
15. Cover orders are predominantly used in **intraday trading**.
16. They reduce risk by ensuring a **predefined exit point** if the market moves against the trade.
17. COs allow traders to **leverage positions** with controlled risk.
18. The stop-loss in a cover order is **mandatory**, preventing overexposure.
19. Cover orders improve **trading discipline** by automating exit points.
20. Traders can use COs to **maximize profit potential** while limiting losses.
