# ⭐ Exchanges – NSE, BSE, NASDAQ, NYSE

1. Stock exchanges are platforms where buyers and sellers meet to trade securities.
2. They provide liquidity, transparency, and a regulated environment for trading.
3. **NSE (National Stock Exchange)** is one of India’s largest exchanges, founded in 1992.
4. NSE introduced electronic trading in India, replacing traditional open outcry systems.
5. Its flagship index, the **Nifty 50**, represents the top 50 Indian companies by market capitalization.
6. NSE facilitates trading in equities, derivatives, ETFs, and debt instruments.
7. **BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)** is the oldest stock exchange in Asia, established in 1875.
8. BSE’s benchmark index, the **Sensex**, represents 30 major Indian companies.
9. BSE also offers trading in equities, derivatives, mutual funds, and debt securities.
10. NSE and BSE together form the backbone of India’s capital markets.
11. Both exchanges are regulated by **SEBI**, which ensures fair trading practices.
12. NSE is known for its advanced electronic trading system and high liquidity.
13. BSE is known for its historical significance and broad investor reach.
14. **NASDAQ** is a major U.S.-based exchange, founded in 1971, primarily for technology stocks.
15. NASDAQ stands for **National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations**.
16. Companies like Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, and Google are listed on NASDAQ.
17. NASDAQ pioneered electronic trading globally, eliminating the need for a physical trading floor.
18. Its index, the **NASDAQ Composite**, tracks over 3,000 listed securities.
19. NASDAQ is considered tech-heavy, influencing global technology investment trends.
20. **NYSE (New York Stock Exchange)** is the largest and oldest U.S. exchange, founded in 1792.
21. It lists companies like Coca-Cola, IBM, and ExxonMobil, spanning multiple industries.
22. NYSE uses a hybrid model with electronic and traditional floor trading.
23. Its index, the **Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA)**, tracks 30 major U.S. companies.
24. NYSE is seen as a symbol of stability, large-cap companies, and market reliability.
25. Stock exchanges ensure **price discovery**, where the market determines fair value.
26. Exchanges also guarantee settlement of trades, ensuring ownership transfer of shares.
27. They maintain a **trading infrastructure** with advanced software, surveillance, and reporting.
28. Exchanges provide indices that act as benchmarks for market performance.
29. NSE’s Nifty and BSE’s Sensex are widely used by investors to gauge Indian market trends.
30. NASDAQ Composite and NYSE indices reflect U.S. economic and corporate health.
31. Exchanges regulate trading hours; NSE and BSE operate from 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM IST.
32. U.S. exchanges like NASDAQ and NYSE operate from 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM EST.
33. Exchanges offer derivatives markets, allowing trading in futures and options.
34. They enable hedging, speculation, and arbitrage strategies.
35. Listing requirements on exchanges include minimum capital, profit history, and corporate governance.
36. Exchanges help maintain investor confidence by ensuring transparency and fair practices.
37. They provide **market data**, including real-time quotes, historical prices, and trading volumes.
38. Exchanges also facilitate **IPO listings**, allowing companies to raise capital from the public.
39. Market participants like brokers, traders, and investors rely on exchanges for execution.
40. Exchanges manage **clearing and settlement**, typically through a central depository.
41. NSE uses **NSDL and CDSL** for dematerialized shares in India.
42. NYSE and NASDAQ use DTCC for clearing and settlement of U.S. securities.
43. Exchanges also enforce rules to prevent **market manipulation** and insider trading.
44. High-frequency trading operates through exchange platforms, requiring speed and reliability.
45. Exchanges often provide investor education, market updates, and research reports.
46. They act as a bridge between companies seeking capital and investors seeking returns.
47. Globally, stock exchanges influence each other due to interconnected financial markets.
48. Events in NYSE or NASDAQ often affect NSE and BSE the following day.
49. Investors monitor multiple exchanges to make informed trading and investment decisions.
50. In summary, exchanges like NSE, BSE, NASDAQ, and NYSE are pillars of financial markets, ensuring liquidity, transparency, and efficient capital allocation.
